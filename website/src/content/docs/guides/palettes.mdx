Every dysonsphere palette is built in [Oklab](https://bottosson.github.io/posts/oklab/) and
resampled by arc length, so sequential ramps step evenly in perceived lightness and diverging ramps
balance around a neutral midpoint. Pass a palette name anywhere a `palette=` argument is accepted,
or pull colors directly with `ds.palette(name, n)`.

## Using a palette

`ds.palette(name, n)` returns `n` evenly spaced stops; `start`, `end`, `step`, and `reverse` give
finer control. Feed the result into any Altair `scale=alt.Scale(range=...)`:

## Choosing a colormap

Every dysonsphere sequential ramp steps evenly in perceived lightness, survives greyscale
conversion, and stays ordered under simulated deuteranopia and protanopia - invariants the test
suite enforces, not just properties the recipes promise. Which one to reach for:

- **`australis`** - the built-in default for continuous color (`config.range.heatmap` and
  `.ramp`): a ~190° journey from deep magenta-wine through violet, cobalt, and teal to luminous
  emerald. The most uniform ramp in the collection (adjacent-step ratio 1.03; viridis is 1.05).
- **`borealis`** - australis without the magenta fringe: rich violet floor to cobalt,
  azure-teal, and emerald. The closest analogue to viridis, and more protanopia-robust than
  viridis itself.
- **`cosmos`** - near-black floor rising to a saturated pink: maximum dynamic range, at home on
  dark backgrounds.
- **`nebula`** - the gentle sibling: slate-teal to pale rose with an airy near-white top, for
  figures that shouldn't shout.
- **`brass` / `pewter`** - deep navy through a near-neutral middle to vivid gold (brass) or warm
  platinum (pewter). The blue-gold axis survives dichromacy completely, making these the most
  colorblind-conservative choice - brass when the figure can carry a warm accent, pewter when it
  can't.
- **Single-hue ramps** (`blues`, `greens`, `pinks`, ...) - when color encodes only intensity and
  hue is doing another job elsewhere in the figure.

For signed or reference-centered data use a 13-stop diverging palette (`pinksblues`,
`redsblues`, ...) and center the scale domain so the white pivot lands on the reference value.

Set the defaults for every chart with `theme(heatmapPalette=..., rampPalette=...)` (see the
[theming guide](/guides/theming/)), or restyle one chart via
`scale=alt.Scale(range=ds.palette(...))`.

## Qualitative categories

`ds.categorical(members=n)` returns the CVD-robust qualitative palette (blue / pink / yellow /
green) sized to your group count. The default (`members=1`) is a **flat, tier-major** palette -
adjacent categories differ in hue, for unrelated groups:

<Example name="palette_categorical" alt="Strip plot colored with the categorical palette" />

For **paired data** (a treatment measured pre/post, two timepoints per condition), pass
`members=2..10`: the palette becomes **hue-major**, so each consecutive block of `members`
categories is one hue climbing in lightness. Sort the members adjacent and each group reads as a
family:

## Browsing the catalogue

The [Palette browser](/palettes/) shows all 300+ palettes as filterable swatch strips, with a
live preview - click any swatch to copy its hex codes and see the charts restyled with it.

## Illustrator swatches

`ds.export_swatches()` writes an Illustrator ExtendScript and an `.ase` swatch file for the whole
catalogue (or a subset), so the exact colors are available in your vector editor. See the
[Palettes reference](/reference/palettes/).
