`add_comparisons()` and `add_correlation()` compute statistics with scipy and draw them as layers.
Every call also builds a structured report - descriptives, exact p-values, and effect sizes - that
`save()` embeds in the export metadata, so a figure carries its own analysis. Pass `report=True` to
print it, or `save=True` to write a text file.

## Group comparisons

`add_comparisons()` classifies `test=` into **pairwise** (`mannwhitneyu`, `ttest_ind`, `ttest_rel`,
`wilcoxon`, `tukey_hsd`) or **omnibus** (`anova`, `kruskal`, `friedman`, `alexandergovern`). Here an
ANOVA with a corner label plus two post-hoc brackets. The corner label sits at the top of the plot,
so pad the y domain and lift the brackets with `yStart=` to keep everything clear:

<Example name="comparisons" alt="Fuel economy by origin with an ANOVA label and post-hoc brackets" />

### Pairwise tests and corrections

Give a list of pairs; brackets stack automatically. `correction=` applies Holm or Bonferroni
multiple-comparison adjustment.

<Example name="comparisons_pairwise" alt="Three pairwise Mann-Whitney brackets with Holm correction" />

### Label styles

`labelStyle="asterisks"` renders `*` / `**` / `***` / `ns`; `bracketStyle="line"` drops the end
ticks.

<Example name="comparisons_asterisks" alt="Pairwise comparison with asterisk labels and line brackets" />

### Number notation

`notation=` controls the p-value format (`"scientific"`, `"e"`, `"power"`), and `sigFigs=` sets the
precision.

<Example name="comparisons_notation" alt="Comparison bracket with scientific-notation p-value" />

Both `bracketStyle` and `notation` also accept a **dict** for per-pair control (keys are pairs,
matched regardless of order); `notation` additionally takes a `"test"` key for the omnibus label:

<Example name="comparisons_perpair" alt="Two brackets with different per-pair styles and notation" />

### Omnibus with post-hoc

For an omnibus test, the corner label reports the omnibus result and the brackets are filled by the
matching post-hoc (Kruskal-Wallis ŌåÆ Dunn here). `omnibusVerbose=True` adds the statistic, degrees of
freedom, and effect size.

<Example name="comparisons_kruskal" alt="Kruskal-Wallis omnibus label with Dunn post-hoc brackets" />

### Grouped (two-factor) comparisons

For a grouped bar chart - a subgroup on `xOffset` within each x-category, like a qPCR gene ├Ś condition
panel - pass `xOffsetCol` to compare the subgroup **levels within each group**. `pairs` names the
levels (defaulted for two); one bracket is drawn per x-category, each with a real per-category test,
sitting above its own group's bars. Set `categories` and `xOffsetSort` to match your chart's `x` /
`xOffset` sort (it raises if they don't cover the data):

<Example name="qpcr" alt="Grouped qPCR bars with a within-gene significance bracket per gene" />

## Correlation

`add_correlation()` adds a coefficient, optional p-value, and (for Pearson) an OLS fit line to a
scatter. The default readout is a bare `r = ŌĆ”`; rank methods report the coefficient only.

<Example name="correlation" alt="Horsepower versus miles per gallon with an OLS fit line" />

For Pearson, `ci=True` shades a confidence band around the fit line (pass a level like `ci=0.99`).
`interval="confidence"` (the default) shows how tightly the line itself is pinned down;
`interval="prediction"` shows the wider band for a single new observation. The band is drawn under
the line and styled with `ciColor` / `ciOpacity`:

<Example name="correlation_ci" alt="OLS fit line with a shaded 95% confidence band" />

The readout is composed from independent parts, so you can show exactly what you want:
`coefficient="both"` gives r and r┬▓, `includePvalue=True` adds P, `includeEquation=True` adds the
fit line's equation, and `verbose=True` is a shortcut that turns all of them on. The longer the
readout, the wider the chart should be (`theme(chartWidth=ŌĆ”)`):

<Example name="correlation_both" alt="Scatter with an r, r-squared, and p-value readout" />

Rank correlations (`method="spearman"` / `"kendall"`) report only the coefficient (Žü / Žä) and its
p-value - no fit line, since a straight line is not their model:

<Example name="correlation_spearman" alt="Weight versus miles per gallon, Spearman coefficient" />

### Per-series correlation

Pass `groupCol` to fit and report a correlation **per group** - one fit line, CI band, and coefficient
per level, each coloured to match its points (colour your scatter by the same field). Colour is a
lookup rather than a position, so no sort parameter is needed. The readouts stack in the corner, one
per series:
