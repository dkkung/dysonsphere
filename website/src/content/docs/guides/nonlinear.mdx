Vega rounds SVG tick positions to integers, which makes minor ticks on nonlinear axes drift
visibly at print DPI. dysonsphere adds correctly spaced minor ticks and fixes the rounding in the
exported SVG.

## Log axes

`add_log_ticks()` adds minor ticks to a log-scale axis (base 10 or any integer base). It wraps your
chart and shares its scale.

<Example name="log" alt="Log-scale line chart of stock prices with minor ticks" />

## Power axes

`add_pow_ticks()` does the same for power and square-root scales, interpolating minor ticks evenly
in transformed space. Pass the same `majorValues` you give the main axis.

<Example name="nonlinear_pow" alt="Square-root axis scatter with evenly spaced minor ticks" />

## Typeset labels

`log_label_expr()` returns a Vega `labelExpr` for superscript log labels (`10¹`, `10²`, …), in
`"power"`, `"scientific"`, `"e"`, or `"si"` notation.

<Example name="nonlinear_labels" alt="Log-scale chart with typeset power-notation labels" />

These exponents are typeset by the same mechanism that handles superscripts and subscripts in any
label - so you can also write `x^2` or `C__t` in an axis title or annotation. See
[Superscripts and subscripts](/guides/text-labels/#superscripts-and-subscripts).
