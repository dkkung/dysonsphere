Unlike the rest of the gallery - which is synthetic showpieces - these are **real figures from the
history of science**, rebuilt with dysonsphere from open or published data. Each is a *reconstruction*
(our binning, our fit, our styling), not the original published figure, and each caption cites where
the data came from. Press **`</> code`** to read the code, or **Open in studio** to run and edit it live.

## Astronomy

<Example name="hubble_1929" codeToggle inwardTicks caption="Hubble's 1929 diagram - the expanding universe. ## Biology

<Example name="mendel_peas" codeToggle caption="Mendel's peas - the 3:1 ratio that founded genetics. F2 counts for seven pea traits: the dominant phenotype turns up in about three-quarters of offspring and the recessive in one-quarter, revealing genes as discrete, independently-segregating units. Each bar is the observed dominant fraction; the line marks the 0.75 expectation. <Example name="ld_fluctuation" codeToggle caption="Luria–Delbrück fluctuation test - proof that mutations arise at random, before selection. Parallel bacterial cultures scored for virus-resistant colonies: an 'induced' hypothesis predicts a tight Poisson, but real counts fluctuate wildly with rare 'jackpots' - the signature of spontaneous mutations seeding early, large clones. Each point is a simulated culture under the two hypotheses (matched in mean); the effect is from <a href='https://doi.org/10.1093/genetics/28.6.491'>Luria & Delbrück, Genetics (1943)</a>." alt="Strip plot of resistant-colony counts: spontaneous cultures fluctuate with jackpots, induced cultures cluster tightly" />
